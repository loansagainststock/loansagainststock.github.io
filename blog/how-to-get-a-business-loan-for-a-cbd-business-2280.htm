How to Get a Business Loan for a CBD Business

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

The CBD industry is booming, with projections estimating it could reach $47 billion by 2028. Entrepreneurs are flocking to this space, but one major hurdle remains: securing financing. Traditional lenders are often hesitant due to the legal gray areas surrounding hemp-derived products. However, with the right strategy, you can secure a business loan to launch or grow your CBD venture.

Understanding the Challenges of CBD Business Financing

Before diving into loan options, it’s crucial to recognize why funding a CBD business is uniquely difficult.

Federal vs. State Regulations

While the 2018 Farm Bill legalized hemp-derived CBD at the federal level, the FDA still restricts its use in food, beverages, and supplements. This regulatory uncertainty makes banks and credit unions wary.

High-Risk Industry Label

Many financial institutions classify CBD businesses as "high-risk," similar to casinos or adult entertainment. This leads to higher interest rates, stricter terms, or outright denials.

Payment Processing Issues

Even if you secure a loan, finding a reliable payment processor can be a challenge. Many traditional processors avoid CBD companies due to chargeback risks.

Types of Business Loans for CBD Companies

Despite these obstacles, several financing options exist. Here’s a breakdown of the most viable ones.

1. Alternative Online Lenders

Online lenders like Kabbage, OnDeck, or Fundbox are more flexible than traditional banks. They often focus on cash flow rather than industry type.

Pros:
- Faster approval times (sometimes within 24 hours).
- Less stringent eligibility requirements.

Cons:
- Higher interest rates.
- Shorter repayment terms.

2. Merchant Cash Advances (MCAs)

An MCA provides a lump sum in exchange for a percentage of future sales.

Pros:
- No collateral required.
- Approval is based on sales volume, not credit score.

Cons:
- Extremely high fees (effective APRs can exceed 100%).
- Daily repayments can strain cash flow.

3. Private Investors or Venture Capital

If you have a scalable CBD brand, equity financing might be the way to go.

Pros:
- No monthly repayments (investors take equity instead).
- Access to mentorship and industry connections.

Cons:
- Giving up partial ownership.
- Lengthy negotiation process.

4. Credit Unions or Community Banks

Some local financial institutions are more open to CBD lending, especially in hemp-friendly states like Colorado or Oregon.

Pros:
- Lower interest rates than online lenders.
- Personalized service.

Cons:
- Strict eligibility criteria.
- Limited funding amounts.

5. Crowdfunding

Platforms like Kickstarter or WeFunder allow you to raise capital from supporters.

Pros:
- No debt or equity loss.
- Market validation through early backers.

Cons:
- Requires strong marketing efforts.
- Not guaranteed (if you don’t hit the goal, you get nothing).

Steps to Improve Your Chances of Approval

Now that you know your options, here’s how to position your CBD business for loan approval.

Build a Solid Business Plan

Lenders want to see:
- Market research (target audience, competition).
- Revenue projections (realistic, data-backed).
- Compliance strategy (how you’ll navigate regulations).

Maintain Excellent Credit

A FICO score above 680 improves your chances. If your credit is weak, consider:
- Paying down existing debt.
- Correcting errors on your report.

Keep Impeccable Financial Records

Lenders will scrutinize:
- Bank statements (consistent revenue is key).
- Tax returns (no discrepancies).
- Expense reports (prove you manage cash flow well).

Establish Relationships with CBD-Friendly Banks

Some financial institutions, like Safe Harbor Financial or Partner Colorado Credit Union, specialize in hemp businesses. Open an account early to build trust.

Consider Collateral

If you’re applying for an SBA loan or traditional bank loan, collateral (real estate, equipment) can strengthen your application.

Avoiding Common Pitfalls

Many CBD entrepreneurs make these mistakes—don’t fall into the same traps.

Misrepresenting Your Business

Never hide the nature of your business. Lenders will discover the truth during underwriting, leading to instant rejection.

Choosing the Wrong Loan Type

An MCA might seem easy, but the crushing repayment terms could sink your business. Always compare options.

Ignoring State Laws

Even if federal law allows CBD, some states impose additional restrictions. Non-compliance can void your loan agreement.

The Future of CBD Financing

As the industry matures, financing options will expand. The SAFE Banking Act, if passed, could open doors to traditional lending. Until then, persistence and creativity are your best tools.

By understanding the challenges, exploring all funding avenues, and presenting a professional application, you can secure the capital needed to thrive in this lucrative market.
